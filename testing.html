Online Assesment for Autism


Some Instructions before you take the test


1. When answering the above questions please consider how much the statements apply to you.


2. Ensure that all questions are answered. Incomplete responses may affect the accuracy of the assessment.


3. This test is for informational purposes only. For a formal diagnosis or if there are concerns about autism, please consult a qualified healthcare professional with the guidance of your supervising adult.


4. Your responses are confidential and will be used only for the purpose of assessment. We adhere to strict privacy and data protection standards.







Test Questions

The above 30 questions may be useful to understand if you are experiencing some of the common behaviours and thoughts associated with being on the Autistic Spectrum. The questions are based on an evidence-based screening tool – the Autism Spectrum Quotient – but are indicative only and do not form a formal diagnosis.
References: S. Baron-Cohen, S. Wheelwright, R. Skinner, J. Martin and E. Clubley, (2001) The Autism Spectrum Quotient (AQ) : Evidence from Asperger Syndrome/High Functioning Autism, Males and Females, Scientists and Mathematicians Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders 31:5-17
